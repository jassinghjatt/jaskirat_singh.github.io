This project documents a detailed literature presentation on the landmark paper **"Entanglement-Assisted Classical Capacity of Noisy Quantum Channels"** by Bennett, Shor, Smolin and Thapliyal (Physical Review Letters, 1999).

The presentation explores one of the central questions in quantum information theory:

> **How much classical information can be transmitted through a noisy quantum channel when the sender and receiver share prior quantum entanglement?**

The discussion develops the foundations of quantum channel capacities before introducing the concept of **entanglement-assisted classical capacity**. It explains how pre-shared Bell states can substantially enhance classical communication over noisy quantum channels without violating causality.

Major topics covered include:

- Classical and quantum channel capacities
- Shannon capacity and mutual information
- Quantum channels and completely positive trace-preserving (CPTP) maps
- Superdense coding
- Quantum teleportation
- Depolarizing and quantum erasure channels
- Entanglement-assisted classical capacity (CE)
- Capacity bounds using superdense coding and teleportation
- Exact capacity expressions for depolarizing and erasure channels
- General quantum mutual information formula for CE

The presentation also discusses the physical significance of the result that shared entanglement can dramatically increase classical communication capacity, even for highly noisy quantum channels where quantum information transmission becomes impossible.
