# Entanglement-Assisted Classical Capacity of Noisy Quantum Channels

This page contains the report, poster and presentation prepared as part of a group study on the paper **"Entanglement-Assisted Classical Capacity of Noisy Quantum Channels"** by Bennett, Shor, Smolin and Thapliyal.

The work focuses on one of the fundamental problems in quantum communication:

> **Understanding how shared quantum entanglement enhances the transmission of classical information through noisy quantum channels.**

The report begins with the foundations of classical information theory before introducing quantum channels and the various notions of channel capacity. It then develops the theoretical framework behind entanglement-assisted communication using superdense coding and quantum teleportation, eventually deriving the exact capacity expressions for depolarizing and quantum erasure channels.

Topics discussed include:

- Information theory and Shannon capacity
- Classical versus quantum channels
- Multiple notions of quantum channel capacity
- Entropy and mutual information
- Superdense coding
- Quantum teleportation
- Depolarizing channels
- Quantum erasure channels
- Exact derivation of entanglement-assisted classical capacity
- Physical interpretation of quantum communication limits

The project consists of a comprehensive technical report, an accompanying scientific poster, and an oral presentation summarizing the principal theoretical ideas and mathematical derivations.
