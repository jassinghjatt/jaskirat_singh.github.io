Ancilla-Assisted Protection of Information: Application to Atom–Cavity Systems

This project presents a technical report, scientific poster, and oral presentation based on the 2023 paper “Ancilla-Assisted Protection of Information: Application to Atom–Cavity Systems” by Gangwar et al.

The work investigates a fundamentally different approach to quantum error protection. Rather than correcting errors after they occur, the protocol transfers quantum information from an atomic system into a decoherence-free subspace encoded in two ancillary photons before environmental noise acts on the atom. After the noisy evolution, the information is deterministically recovered, effectively making an arbitrary noisy environment transparent to the protected quantum state.

Major topics covered include:

  • Open quantum systems and environmental decoherence
  • Limitations of conventional quantum error correction
  • Decoherence-free subspaces (DFS)
  • Ancilla-assisted quantum information protection
  • Atom–cavity quantum electrodynamics (QED)
  • Three-level atomic systems
  • Controlled atom-photon interactions
  • STIRAP-based quantum control
  • Optical quantum memories
  • Noise transparency through unitary encoding and decoding
  • Experimental implementation using cavity QED

The report provides a detailed derivation of the protocol, beginning from the physical atom–cavity setup and proceeding through the complete mathematical evolution of the quantum state using density matrices, unitary transformations, and quantum channels. It also discusses practical implementation challenges including photon loss, gate fidelities, and timing constraints in realistic cavity-QED experiments.
